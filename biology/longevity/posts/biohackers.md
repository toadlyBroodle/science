# Update: 87 longevity papers later, here's what the evidence actually supports

I run an open-source wiki of peer-reviewed primary papers across longevity, rejuvenation, and preventive medicine. After integrating feedback from the original post, the catalog now sits at 87 sources / 146 wiki pages, every paper tagged with an evidence tier (T0 in vitro through T7 hard-endpoint RCT or meta-analysis).

This is a corrected and expanded version of the earlier post. Errors flagged in the comments have been fixed at source: the Helgerud 4x4 figure is 7.2% in moderately trained subjects, not 13%; the PEARL rapamycin trial primary endpoint (visceral adiposity, p=0.379) was not met and the headline secondary findings are sex-stratified subgroup results; the 2026 ACC/AHA dyslipidemia guideline now formally recognizes ApoB. Thanks to the commenters who caught those.

The wiki separates two things that the original post conflated: **biomarkers to measure** vs **interventions to do**. Training your grip strength does not extend your life. Strong grip is a marker of being healthy, not a cause of longevity. Resistance training is the cause; grip is one of the markers that tracks it. Same separation applies to VO2max, ApoB, waist-to-hip, and so on.

## Interventions with hard-endpoint evidence

These move all-cause mortality more than any compound currently sold to the public.

- **Aerobic / cardio training** (T7). Mandsager 2018, n=122,007: low-vs-elite cardiorespiratory fitness, HR 5.04 for all-cause mortality, no upper limit of benefit. Helgerud 2007 4x4 protocol raises VO2max ~7.2% over 8 weeks in moderately trained males; untrained beginners typically see 10-15%. The "+13%" number that gets quoted online is from secondary summaries that conflated this study with later trials in untrained or clinical populations.

- **Resistance training** (T7). Saeidifard 2019 meta-analysis: ~21% lower all-cause mortality alone, ~40% combined with aerobic exercise. Adding **creatine monohydrate 5 g/day** to RT in older adults (Chilibeck 2017 meta-analysis, 22 RCTs, n=721) adds +1.37 kg lean mass and significantly larger 1RM gains over RT alone. Creatine is the cleanest supplement-aisle case in the wiki: T7 evidence on the same biomarkers (lean mass, strength) the wiki uses to justify resistance training itself.

- **Sleep 7-9 hr** (T7 short-sleep arm). Cappuccio 2010 meta-analysis, n~1.4M: short sleep <6 hr RR 1.12, long sleep >9 hr RR 1.30. Short-sleep effect is more clearly causal; long sleep partly reflects underlying illness.

- **Smoking, alcohol** (T7). Jha 2013: smoking costs >10 years of life. Wood 2018, n=599,912 drinkers: lowest mortality at ~100 g ethanol/week; the "moderate drinking is protective" finding largely vanishes once sick quitters are excluded from the never-drinker comparator.

- **Lower BP to ~120 SBP** (T7). SPRINT 2015, n=9,361: intensive vs standard target cut all-cause mortality 27%. Trial halted early for benefit.

- **Lower ApoB / LDL-C with statins where indicated** (T7). CTT 2012 IPD meta-analysis of 27 RCTs: each 1 mmol/L LDL-C reduction yields ~21% lower vascular events per year. The honest framing: benefit is real and depends on baseline CV risk; absolute risk reduction in primary prevention is modest (NNT in the hundreds); side-effect frequency is real but smaller than the popular literature implies. Discuss with a physician using your individual 10-year risk estimate.

- **Measure ApoB, not just LDL-C**. Sniderman 2011 meta-analysis: ApoB beats LDL-C by ~12% on relative-risk-reduction prediction. The 2026 ACC/AHA dyslipidemia guideline now formally recognizes that LDL-C and ApoB give differing risk estimates and that clinically significant discordance (LDL-C at goal but ApoB elevated) signals persistent atherogenic burden.

- **Measure Lp(a) once in a lifetime**. Kamstrup 2009 Mendelian randomization across three Copenhagen cohorts: genetically elevated Lp(a) doubles MI risk independently of LDL-C and ApoB. ~20% of the population has elevated Lp(a). Set largely at birth; one-time measurement suffices. No approved drug specifically lowers it yet (HORIZON, OCEAN(a), ACCLAIM-Lp(a) phase 3 trials are pending). If yours is elevated, the actionable response is more aggressive optimization of every modifiable risk factor.

- **GLP-1 agonists in obesity with prior CVD** (T7). SELECT trial 2023, n=17,604, semaglutide 2.4 mg/week vs placebo, ~40 month follow-up: HR 0.80 on the primary MACE endpoint. ~17% discontinued for adverse events (mostly GI). Pushes GLP-1s out of "diabetes / weight-loss" framing into cardiometabolic prevention.

- **Treat documented deficiencies; don't megadose**. VITAL 2019, n=25,871: vitamin D 2000 IU/day in non-deficient adults was null on cancer (HR 0.96), CVD (HR 0.97), and all-cause mortality (HR 0.99). Test before treating.

## Indication-specific (only if you have the indication)

- **CoQ10 100 mg three times daily for symptomatic chronic heart failure** (T7 in HF only). Q-SYMBIO 2014, n=420 NYHA III-IV patients: primary MACE HR 0.50, all-cause mortality HR 0.51 over 2 years. HF-specific result; no comparable RCT in healthy adults.

- **Hormone replacement therapy in postmenopausal women** (T7 mortality-neutral). Manson 2017, 18-year follow-up of the WHI RCTs, n=27,347: all-cause mortality HR 0.99. The 2002 early termination drove a worldwide collapse in HRT prescribing on the basis of relative-risk increases that were small in absolute terms. Modern transdermal-estradiol-with-micronized-progesterone protocols are a different risk-benefit profile from the WHI's oral CEE+MPA. For symptomatic women near menopause, modern HRT is reasonable; for asymptomatic longevity, it is not RCT-supported.

- **CPAP** is highly effective for daytime sleepiness and quality of life. SAVE 2016, n=2,687 OSA patients with prior CVD: CPAP did not reduce hard CV events (HR 1.10, p=0.34). Treat sleep apnea for the symptoms; the cardiovascular-prevention indication is not RCT-supported despite the clinical-assumption default.

## Promising but not RCT-validated for longevity

- **Rapamycin** (T3 in mice; T6 with primary endpoint not met in humans). The PEARL trial 2025 primary efficacy endpoint (visceral adiposity) was not met (p=0.379). Significant healthspan effects emerged only in sex-stratified subgroup analyses of secondary endpoints: lean tissue mass and pain in women on 10 mg, bone mineral content in men on 10 mg. Safety endpoint was met (well tolerated over 48 weeks). Mouse evidence remains the strongest case (+20-25% in UM-HET3, ITP-grade replicated). Off-label use exists; honest framing is "promising mouse evidence with first human trial that did not meet its primary endpoint."

- **Sauna** (T6 observational). Laukkanen 2015, Finnish KIHD cohort, n=2,315 men, 20.7-year follow-up. 4-7 sauna sessions/week vs 1/week: HR 0.50 fatal CVD, HR 0.37 sudden cardiac death, HR 0.60 all-cause mortality. Observational; healthy-user bias plausible. Effect size is similar to high-leverage exercise interventions but the underlying evidence is observational rather than RCT.

- **Urolithin A (Mitopure)** (T6 RCT, modest effect on immune endpoints).

## Interesting but weak evidence

- **Glucosamine**. Ma 2019 UK Biobank, n=466,039: HR 0.85 CVD events. Observational only; no RCT confirmation. The popular claim that glucosamine has better data than vitamin D inverts the evidence hierarchy: vitamin D had a clean negative phase-3 RCT; glucosamine has only large prospective cohort data.

- **Cold exposure**. Buijze 2016 RCT, n=3,018: 29% reduction in self-reported sickness absence over 30 days. No change in actual illness days. Soft endpoint; unblinded. Brown-fat / cold-shock-protein mechanisms are plausible but not endpoint-validated. 30 seconds at the end of a regular shower is the simplest evidence-positive protocol; longer durations did not help.

## Don't bother

- **NMN / NR alone**. Blood NAD+ rises; clinical endpoints don't. NR in long-COVID 2025 is the latest large RCT and the pattern persists.
- **Universal vitamin D in non-deficient adults** (per VITAL).
- **Whole "young plasma" without a defined active fraction**. Plasmapheresis-without-IVIG was negative in a 2025 RCT (Horvath co-author); some clocks accelerated. The TPE+IVIG protocol that worked (-2.6 yr biological age) suggests the active fraction is the IVIG, not the plasma removal.
- **Telomerase as a pill**. The 2022 PNAS MCMV-TERT paper with the +41% lifespan headline was retracted in August 2025. Tert-knockin mouse evidence is real but is gene therapy, not a supplement.
- **Most "longevity stacks"** (resveratrol, anti-aging peptides, exotic herbal blends): no RCT support at endpoint level.

## What's exciting in the lab

- Anti-IL-11 antibody: +22-25% mouse lifespan late-life. Already in human trials for fibrotic lung disease.
- Trametinib + rapamycin combo: +27-29% mouse lifespan additive over rapamycin alone, both drugs FDA-approved separately. Trametinib monotherapy has serious cancer-drug toxicity, so wait for a phase-2 combination trial.
- AAV-Klotho gene therapy: +20% mouse lifespan with one shot.
- CAR-T senolytics: single infusion persists >12 months in mice, generalizing from metabolism into gut aging.
- Partial reprogramming (NewLimit, Retro Bio, Altos) heading toward first-in-human entries.

The wiki tracks pending readouts that will reshape this list at `wiki/analysis/yet-to-publish.md`: HORIZON / OCEAN(a) / ACCLAIM-Lp(a) for Lp(a) lowering, SURMOUNT-MMO for tirzepatide CV, the trametinib + rapamycin Phase 2 if it materializes, klotho s-KL Phase 1, and partial-reprogramming first-in-human entries.
