# Deep aging clocks: AI-powered strategies for biological age estimation

## Summary
2025 review in *Ageing Research Reviews* surveying DNN-based biological-age
estimators (deep [[topics/aging-clocks]]) across modalities.

## Three generations of clocks
1. **1st gen** — trained solely on chronological age (Horvath, Hannum).
2. **2nd gen** — incorporates phenotypic / mortality info (PhenoAge,
   GrimAge, GrimAge2, pace-of-aging).
3. **3rd gen** — causality-enriched models separating causal from
   correlational age signal (CausAge, DamAge, AdaptAge), plus pan-mammalian
   clocks.

## Multi-modal frontier
Integration of: medical images, biochemical panels, cell counts, and
multi-omics (epigenomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, metabolomics,
microbiome, lipidomics).

## Why a CS person should care
Map of the modeling landscape — tells you what's already been tried
across modalities and where generational advances are still missing.
Especially useful before building yet another clock.
