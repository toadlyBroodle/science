# PEDF — a young-blood rejuvenation factor

## Summary
Identifies **PEDF** (pigment epithelium-derived factor) as a circulating
protein that:
- Extends replicative lifespan of primary human fibroblasts in vitro.
- **Declines with age in mammals.**
- When administered systemically to aged mice, reverses age-related
  functional decline and pathology across multiple tissues.

## Observed effects in aged mice on systemic PEDF
- Improved cognitive function.
- Reduced hepatic fibrosis.

## Why it matters
Adds PEDF to the short list of validated young-blood rejuvenation
factors (alongside GDF11, Klotho, PF4, FGF17). All plausible targets
for recombinant-protein or AAV-based therapies.
