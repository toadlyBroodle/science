# Glucosamine and cardiovascular events in UK Biobank

## Summary
Ma et al. UK Biobank prospective analysis: **n=466,039 adults
without CVD at baseline, ~7-year median follow-up**.
Self-reported habitual glucosamine use at baseline.

After adjustment for age, sex, BMI, race, lifestyle, diet,
medications, and other supplement use:

| Outcome | HR (95% CI) |
|---------|-------------|
| Total CVD events | 0.85 (0.80–0.90) |
| CVD death | 0.78 (0.70–0.87) |
| Coronary heart disease | 0.82 (0.76–0.88) |
| Stroke | 0.91 (0.83–1.00) |

Effects were broadly consistent across age, sex, BMI, smoking,
alcohol, diet, and physical-activity strata.

## Why it matters
Glucosamine is a cheap, OTC, generally well-tolerated supplement
typically taken for joint pain. The UK Biobank signal — if causal —
would put it in a similar effect-size range to low-dose aspirin
for CVD prevention, without aspirin's bleeding risk.

## Key findings
- Total CVD HR 0.85, CVD death HR 0.78, CHD HR 0.82.
- Stroke HR 0.91 (CI just touches 1.00).
- Dose-response not directly assessed (binary "habitual use"
  variable).

## Caveats
- **Observational, not RCT.** Glucosamine users in UK Biobank are
  systematically more health-conscious: more exercise, better diet,
  less smoking, more frequent doctor visits. Despite extensive
  adjustment, residual confounding by health-conscious lifestyle is
  the main alternative explanation.
- A 2023 Mendelian-randomisation analysis on UK Biobank glucosamine
  and incident heart failure (Mayo Clin Proc) provides some
  triangulation but not a clean causal identification.
- No phase-3 RCT of glucosamine for cardiovascular prevention exists;
  the few RCTs in osteoarthritis populations are powered for joint
  outcomes, not CVD.
- The Reddit claim that "glucosamine has much better data than
  vitamin D" is **overstated**: vitamin D had a clean negative phase-3
  RCT ([[papers/vital-2019-vitd-omega3]], n=25,871, 5.3y); glucosamine
  has only large prospective cohort data without RCT confirmation.
  The two evidence bases are at different tiers.

## Practical takeaway
- If you already take glucosamine for joint pain, the cohort signal
  is mild reassurance that it isn't harming cardiovascular endpoints
  and may modestly lower them.
- If you are deciding from scratch on cardiovascular grounds alone,
  the absence of an RCT means this is a Tier 6 observational signal
  to file under "interesting but unconfirmed."
- Don't self-prescribe based on UK-Biobank-style observational
  findings without considering that **most** observational
  associations in supplement literature have failed to replicate in
  RCTs (vitamin D, vitamin E, beta-carotene, fish oil for primary
  prevention all followed this pattern).
