# Intensive BP control (target <120) cuts mortality by 27% (SPRINT)

## Summary
**SPRINT** was a randomized trial in **9,361 non-diabetic
hypertensive adults** at elevated CV risk, comparing intensive SBP
target **<120 mm Hg** vs standard **<140 mm Hg**. Trial **halted
early** (median 3.26 yr) for clear benefit. Intensive control reduced
the primary composite (MI, ACS, stroke, HF, CV death) by **25%**
(HR 0.75) and **all-cause mortality by 27%** (HR 0.73). Adverse
events (hypotension, syncope, AKI) were modestly more common in the
intensive group.

## Why it matters
The single most influential modern hypertension trial. Drove the
2017 ACC/AHA guideline shift that lowered the hypertension threshold
to 130/80 and target to <130/80 (or <120/80 in higher-risk adults).
**Established "go lower than you think" as the modern BP target**
for CV-risk adults, replacing the older 140 / 90 target.

## Key findings
- N = 9,361, mean age 68, ~36% women, ~30% Black.
- Inclusion: SBP 130-180, ≥1 CV risk factor, NO diabetes, NO prior
  stroke.
- Primary composite (MI/ACS/stroke/HF/CV death): **HR 0.75**
  (95% CI 0.64-0.89), p < 0.001.
- All-cause mortality: **HR 0.73** (95% CI 0.60-0.90), p = 0.003.
- HF reduction was the largest individual contributor.
- Intensive-arm AEs: hypotension OR 1.67, syncope OR 1.33, AKI
  OR 1.66 (all small absolute numbers).

## How to act on it
- Home BP cuff is more accurate than clinic; sustained <120/80 is
  the SPRINT-supported target for non-diabetic CV-risk adults.
- Standing BP and renal function need monitoring on intensive
  regimens (especially in older adults).
- The 2021 STEP trial (older Chinese adults) and the SPRINT-MIND
  cognitive substudy reinforce SPRINT.

## Caveats
- Excluded diabetes (the parallel ACCORD trial in diabetics was
  null on the composite); BP target in T2D is more debated.
- Excluded prior stroke.
- Office-BP measurement was unattended automated, which reads ~5-10
  mmHg lower than typical clinic measurement; absolute targets are
  not directly comparable to standard-clinic numbers.
