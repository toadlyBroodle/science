# Sauna bathing and 20-year mortality (Finnish KIHD cohort)

## Summary
Laukkanen et al. prospective analysis of the Finnish Kuopio
Ischaemic Heart Disease Risk Factor (KIHD) cohort: **n=2,315
middle-aged men (42–60 yr), median follow-up 20.7 years**. Sauna
frequency and session duration recorded at baseline.

Compared with men who used a sauna **once per week**:

| Sauna frequency | Sudden cardiac death HR | Fatal CVD HR | All-cause mortality HR |
|-----------------|------------------------|--------------|------------------------|
| 2–3 sessions / week | 0.78 | 0.73 | 0.76 |
| 4–7 sessions / week | **0.37** | **0.50** | **0.60** |

Effects were dose-responsive on both frequency and per-session
duration (HRs lower for sessions >19 min).

## Why it matters
The single largest published mortality effect for any non-pharmacological,
non-exercise intervention in the Reddit-feedback request set. Even
attenuating heavily for confounding, the effect persists across
adjustment for age, BMI, smoking, alcohol, hypertension, prior
CHD, lipids, fitness, and resting BP.

## Key findings
- N = 2,315 men, 20.7 yr median follow-up.
- 190 sudden cardiac deaths, 281 fatal CHD, 407 fatal CVD,
  929 all-cause mortality events.
- 4–7 sauna sessions/week vs 1/week: HR 0.50 fatal CVD, HR 0.37
  sudden cardiac death, HR 0.60 all-cause mortality.
- Per-session duration also dose-responsive: sessions
  >19 minutes were associated with lower CVD mortality vs <11
  minutes.

## Caveats
- **Observational, not randomised.** Healthy-user bias is plausible
  (regular sauna-goers may be healthier in unmeasured ways), though
  the cohort is unusual in that sauna culture is universal in Finland
  and so the comparison is "more sauna" vs "less sauna" rather than
  "any sauna" vs "none."
- **Population is Finnish men, traditional dry Finnish sauna**
  (~80–100 °C, dry heat). Generalisation to infrared saunas, hot
  tubs, or other thermal protocols is not direct.
- Mendelian randomisation is unavailable for sauna exposure, so
  residual confounding cannot be fully ruled out.
- Heat exposure does carry acute risks: hypotension, dehydration,
  arrhythmia in vulnerable individuals. Trial-level cardiovascular
  RCTs of sauna exposure remain limited.

## Practical takeaway
- 4+ sessions per week of ~20 min at traditional sauna temperatures
  is the regimen associated with the largest mortality reduction in
  this cohort.
- Modest rehydration and gradual cooldown reduce the acute risk
  profile.
- Effect size is in the same range as exercise interventions and
  larger than most pharmacological primary-prevention agents at the
  population level — but the underlying evidence is observational,
  not RCT-level.
