# Dietary restriction in 960 genetically diverse mice

## Summary
Systematic test of **graded CR (20% / 40%)** and **intermittent fasting
(1d / 2d per week)** on health and survival of **960 genetically diverse
(Diversity Outbred) female mice**.

## Key findings
- Both CR and IF extend lifespan **in proportion** to degree of restriction.
- Median lifespan: ad-lib 25 mo → IF 28 mo → 80% CR 30 mo → 60% CR 34 mo.
- **Genetics ≈ 3× the effect size of dietary intervention** on lifespan.
- **Stress-resilience traits** (maintained body weight / fat / immune cell
  profile under food stress; no late-life fat loss) best predict long
  lifespan.
- Metabolic markers (weight, fat %, glucose, body temp) **do not** explain
  the CR → lifespan link.
- Immune and red-blood-cell traits are more strongly linked to lifespan.

## Why a CS person should care
Rich public phenotypic dataset + DO-mouse genotypes = a natural setting
for heritability decomposition, causal inference, and multi-omics
integration. "Resilience" signatures are not yet formalized — open ML
problem.
