# ITP: epicatechin, halofuginone, mitoglitazone extend male lifespan

## Summary
NIA Interventions Testing Program (Strong, Harrison, Miller et al.),
GeroScience 2025. In genetically heterogeneous UM-HET3 mice tested
across three sites (Jackson Lab, University of Michigan, UT Health San
Antonio), three new compounds extended median lifespan in males:
epicatechin (+~5%), halofuginone (+~9%), and mitoglitazone (+~9%).
None extended female lifespan.

## Why it matters
ITP results are the gold standard for mouse lifespan evidence
([[papers/itp-nia]]): replicated, multi-site, genetically diverse. These
three positives add distinct chemical classes to the geroprotector
roster beyond the rapamycin/MEK-inhibitor axis
([[papers/trametinib-rapamycin-itp-2025]]). The uniform male-only
benefit continues the strong sex bias seen across ITP hits
([[papers/itp-sex-specific-2025]]), reinforcing that sex is a
first-order modifier of pharmacological lifespan extension.

## Key findings
- Epicatechin: +~5% male median lifespan; also raised 90th-percentile
  (late-life) survival in males.
- Halofuginone: +~9% male median lifespan; also raised 90th-percentile
  male survival. Mechanistically mimics amino-acid/low-protein
  restriction.
- Mitoglitazone: +~9% male median lifespan (insulin-sensitizer class).
- No lifespan benefit in females for any of the three.
- UM-HET3 mice, three-site replication.
