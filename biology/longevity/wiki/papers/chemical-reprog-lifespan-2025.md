# Chemical reprogramming extends lifespan

## Summary
Follow-up to [[papers/yang-chemical-cocktails-2023]]. Demonstrates that
partial chemical reprogramming **ameliorates multiple
[[topics/hallmarks-of-aging]]** at the cellular level and **extends
lifespan in mice**.

## Contribution
- Shows the Sinclair-lab chemical cocktails are not just transcriptomic
  but also phenotypic — hallmark-level damage is reversed.
- First demonstration that a chemical (vs. genetic) reprogramming
  protocol modifies lifespan in a mammal.

## Caveat
See [[papers/lipid-droplets-reprog-2025]] — at higher doses, in-vivo
chemical reprogramming causes lipid-droplet accumulation in liver and
kidney, hindering the rejuvenation effect. This limits the
therapeutic window.
