# Unbiased comparison of 14 epigenetic clocks vs. 174 incident disease outcomes

## Summary
Large-scale (n = **18,859**) independent evaluation of 14 widely-used
epigenetic clocks against 10-year onset of 174 diseases + all-cause
mortality.

## Key findings
- **Generational gap**: 2nd- and 3rd-generation clocks (PhenoAge,
  GrimAge, DunedinPACE) significantly outperform 1st-generation (Horvath,
  Hannum) for disease prediction.
- **176 Bonferroni-significant** clock–disease associations; **27** have
  hazard ratios exceeding the clock's own all-cause mortality HR
  (includes primary lung cancer and diabetes).
- Adding a clock to a traditional-risk-factor classifier improves
  accuracy >1% for **32/176** disease-clock pairs.
- Respiratory and liver diseases are best predicted.
- Minimal interaction with sex or smoking history.

## Why a CS person should care
No single clock dominates — this is a classic "it depends" ML benchmark
result. Gap: a disease-aware meta-model or mixture-of-experts built over
the 14 clocks is unexplored and tractable.
