# Cell-type-resolved plasma proteomic ageing clocks (n=60,542)

## Summary
Ding et al. (Wyss-Coray lab, Stanford), Nature Medicine 2026. Using more
than 7,000 plasma proteins measured in 60,542 individuals, the authors
model the biological age of over 40 distinct cell types from blood
alone, then link each cell type's age to incident disease and mortality
over 15 years of follow-up.

## Why it matters
Moves the wiki's plasma-proteomic clock coverage from organ-level
([[papers/plasma-proteomics-brain-immune-2025]]) to cell-type
resolution. A single blood draw yields per-cell-type biological ages
that carry specific, actionable disease risk, a clear successor rather
than a duplicate. The astrocyte-APOE4 interaction connects peripheral
proteomics to brain ageing risk without imaging or CSF.

## Key findings
- >7,000 plasma proteins in 60,542 individuals model biological age of
  >40 cell types.
- Extreme astrocyte ageing tripled Alzheimer's risk in APOE4/4 carriers;
  youthful astrocytes lowered it.
- Respiratory-cell ageing flagged smokers at 58% higher lung-cancer
  risk; myeloid ageing flagged higher diabetes risk in normoglycemic
  individuals.
- Cell-type ageing signatures predicted incident disease and mortality
  over 15 years.
- Large human cohort, observational (not interventional).
