# Lipid-droplet toxicity from in-vivo chemical reprogramming

## Summary
Critical **negative-result** paper. In UM-HET3 mice ([[papers/itp-nia]]
genetic background), partial chemical reprogramming (Sinclair-style
cocktails) **upregulates intracellular lipid droplets** in liver and
kidney — a toxicity that **hinders rejuvenation**.

## Experimental findings
- At **low dose** (implantable osmotic pump), cocktail is safe but has
  **no effect** on transcriptomic age in kidney/liver and only a
  modest effect on OXPHOS.
- At **high dose**, the cocktail causes drastic loss in body weight
  and body condition.
- Oil-red-O staining shows excessive lipid droplets in liver + kidney.

## Mitochondrial findings (in vitro)
Chemical reprogramming in aged fibroblasts enlarges mitochondria,
alters cristae, fuses networks, and accelerates movement velocity —
i.e. partial reversal of aging mitochondrial phenotypes. But the
in-vivo story is complicated by the lipid droplet issue.

## Why this matters
Hard negative result on a widely-hyped therapeutic direction. Any CS
project scoring in-vivo rejuvenation endpoints should include lipid
droplet / hepatic steatosis signatures as safety readouts.
