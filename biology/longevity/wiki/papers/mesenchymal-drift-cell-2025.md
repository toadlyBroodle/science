# Mesenchymal drift reversed by partial reprogramming

## Summary
Introduces **"mesenchymal drift"** (MD) as a pervasive aging/disease
signature — coordinated upregulation of mesenchymal genes across cell
types plus altered stromal composition.

## Scale
- >40 human tissues analyzed
- 20 diseases compared
- In-vivo validation in naturally aged mice

## Key findings
- Higher MD correlates with disease progression, **reduced patient
  survival**, and elevated mortality.
- Suppression of key MD transcription factors → **epigenetic
  rejuvenation**.
- **Yamanaka-factor partial reprogramming reduces MD before
  dedifferentiation** / pluripotency — the rejuvenation precedes the
  cell-fate change.
- Even cells that **cannot** be fully reprogrammed still suppress MD —
  suggesting an MD-directed mechanism independent of pluripotency.
- Naturally aged mice with long-term (7+ month) OSKM induction show
  decreased MD expression in kidney, liver, and most organs.

## Why a CS person should care
Underlying analysis rests on public bulk/single-cell expression (GTEx,
TCGA, Tabula Muris Senis, dozens of partial-reprogramming GEO series).
Reimplementing MD-score and stress-testing it across untested cohorts
is tractable. Scoring individual patients on MD could become a
clinical biomarker.
