# Q-SYMBIO: CoQ10 reduces MACE in chronic heart failure

## Summary
Mortensen et al. **Q-SYMBIO** multicentre RCT: **n=420 chronic heart
failure patients** (NYHA III–IV, mean LVEF ~31%) randomised to
**CoQ10 100 mg three times daily** or placebo on top of standard
guideline-directed therapy. 2-year follow-up.

**Primary composite endpoint** (MACE: unplanned hospitalisation for
worsening HF, CV death, urgent transplant, mechanical circulatory
support): **15% CoQ10 vs 26% placebo, HR 0.50 (p=0.003).**

Secondary:
- **All-cause mortality** 9% vs 17%, HR 0.51 (p=0.018).
- **Cardiovascular mortality** 9% vs 16%, HR 0.43 (p=0.013).
- NYHA class and LVEF improved at 2 yr.

## Why it matters
The cleanest single RCT showing a mortality-grade benefit for CoQ10
in any indication. It is **specific to advanced symptomatic heart
failure** (NYHA III–IV), not a general-population result. CoQ10's
mechanism (mitochondrial electron-transport cofactor; depleted by
statins) plausibly addresses mitochondrial dysfunction characteristic
of failing myocardium.

## Key findings
- Primary MACE HR 0.50 (p=0.003).
- All-cause mortality HR 0.51 (p=0.018) over 2 yr.
- CV mortality HR 0.43 (p=0.013).
- Treatment well tolerated; AE rates similar to placebo.

## Caveats
- **Population is symptomatic chronic HF (NYHA III–IV) on
  background guideline therapy.** Generalisation to healthy
  adults, asymptomatic LV dysfunction, or "longevity in the
  general population" is not supported by this trial.
- Single trial; no comparable replication phase-3 trial yet
  exists. Earlier smaller CoQ10 HF trials were inconsistent;
  Q-SYMBIO is the largest and longest.
- The CoQ10 evidence base outside HF is dominated by
  statin-myalgia trials (mixed) and small surrogate-endpoint
  studies. Mortality-grade evidence outside HF does not exist.
- Form (ubiquinone vs ubidecarenone vs ubiquinol) and dose may
  matter; Q-SYMBIO used ubidecarenone 300 mg/day total. Lower
  doses commonly sold OTC may not reproduce the effect.

## Practical takeaway
- For patients with **symptomatic chronic HF on optimal medical
  therapy**, adding CoQ10 100 mg TID is supported by Q-SYMBIO
  and is safe; discuss with a cardiologist familiar with the
  evidence.
- For **healthy adults**, CoQ10 supplementation lacks
  RCT-level mortality evidence. Mechanism is real but the
  general-population benefit is not demonstrated.
- The Reddit ask for CoQ10 to be addressed in the wiki is fair;
  the honest answer is "T7 mortality-grade evidence in advanced
  heart failure only."
