# CALERIE-2 metabolomics: CR stabilizes carbohydrate intermediates

## Summary
CALERIE Phase 2 investigators, medRxiv 2026. Untargeted
mass-spectrometry metabolomics on fasted plasma at baseline, 12, and 24
months from participants randomized to roughly 25% caloric restriction
versus ad libitum. Principal-components analysis over 864 known
metabolites in 9 super-pathways, with linear mixed models for group by
time effects.

## Why it matters
CALERIE is the canonical human caloric-restriction RCT. Prior readouts
centred on epigenetic pace-of-ageing (DunedinPACE). This ancillary
metabolomic analysis is a fresh computational entry point into the same
trial, complementing the wiki's broader caloric-restriction evidence
([[papers/dr-960-mice-nature-2024]] in mice;
[[papers/lifestyle-atlas-tirolgesund-2025]] in humans). Findings are
secondary/ancillary, not the trial's primary endpoint.

## Key findings
- Untargeted metabolomics on fasted plasma at baseline/12/24 months,
  ~25% CR vs ad libitum.
- 864 known metabolites across 9 super-pathways; PCA plus linear mixed
  models.
- Three components showed significant group-by-time interaction: PC2
  (carbohydrate), PC4 (lipid/sphingolipids), PC5 (partially
  characterized).
- CR stabilized carbohydrate intermediates while the ad libitum arm
  rebounded at 24 months.
