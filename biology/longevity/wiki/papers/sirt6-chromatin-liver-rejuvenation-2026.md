# SIRT6 reverses chromatin ageing in aged mouse liver

## Summary
Cohen et al. (Bar-Ilan University, with NIA and Tel Aviv University),
Nature Communications 2026. Multi-omics profiling (ATAC-seq, methylome,
RNA-seq) shows ageing increases chromatin accessibility in the male
mouse liver, with upregulated inflammation and downregulated metabolism.
SIRT6 overexpression in roughly 24-month-old mice reversed about 80% of
age-related chromatin changes within about one month.

## Why it matters
A non-Yamanaka epigenetic-rejuvenation lever. Where partial
reprogramming uses OSK factors ([[papers/aav-osk-lifespan-2024]],
[[papers/chemical-reprog-lifespan-2025]]), this acts through a single
chromatin regulator (SIRT6) on already-aged wild-type tissue and shows
reversal, not just slowing. It strengthens the case that chromatin state
is a tractable, druggable node in [[topics/hallmarks-of-aging]].

## Key findings
- Ageing raises chromatin accessibility in male mouse liver;
  inflammation up, metabolism down.
- SIRT6 overexpression in ~24-month-old mice reversed ~80% of
  age-related chromatin changes within ~1 month.
- First demonstration of SIRT6 reversing (not just slowing) established
  ageing changes in already-aged animals.
- Reduced inflammation and improved metabolic and liver function.
- Single study, males only; liver-specific.
