# AI as a catalyst in aging research (2026 review)

## Summary
Cross-organism review of ML/DL/computer-vision applications across yeast,
C. elegans, Drosophila, and mice.

## Covers
- Lifespan prediction
- Biomarker + gene discovery
- Aging-clock construction
- Assay automation (animal counting, imaging) via CV

## Gaps flagged (very useful for project selection)
- **Only 3% of reviewed studies included in-vivo biological validation.**
- Common issues: small/imbalanced data, dataset bias, prediction noise,
  poor cross-species generalization, overfitting, lack of experimental
  validation.
- Synthetic-data concerns: may miss real biological noise and rare
  phenotypes; risks introducing bias.

## Why a CS person should care
This is the "here's what's broken" catalog. Any project that explicitly
addresses one of these gaps (e.g., cross-species transfer,
validation-aware benchmarks) is above the field median before it starts.
