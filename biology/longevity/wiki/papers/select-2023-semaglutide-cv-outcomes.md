# SELECT: semaglutide cuts MACE in non-diabetic obese adults

## Summary
Lincoff et al. SELECT trial: **n=17,604, double-blind, placebo-
controlled phase-3 RCT** in adults age 45+ with prior CVD and BMI
≥27 but no diabetes, randomised 1:1 to **semaglutide 2.4 mg/week**
or placebo. Mean exposure 34.2 mo; mean follow-up 39.8 mo.

**Primary composite endpoint** (CV death + nonfatal MI + nonfatal
stroke): **6.5% semaglutide vs 8.0% placebo, HR 0.80 (95% CI
0.72–0.90, p<0.001).** Both fatal and nonfatal MI components
moved in the same direction.

## Why it matters
First phase-3 RCT showing a GLP-1 agonist reduces hard
cardiovascular endpoints in a non-diabetic obese population. Pushes
the GLP-1 class out of "diabetes / weight-loss drug" framing into
"cardiometabolic-prevention drug" territory. Anchors the
[[papers/semaglutide-glp1-epigenetic-age-rct-2025]] biological-age
result with a mortality-adjacent hard endpoint.

## Key findings
- Primary endpoint: HR 0.80, p<0.001.
- Body weight: ~9–10% loss vs ~1% on placebo at the long-term
  follow-up.
- **Adverse events leading to permanent discontinuation: 16.6% on
  semaglutide vs 8.2% on placebo.** Mostly GI (nausea, vomiting,
  diarrhoea); serious AEs were not increased.
- Effect was consistent across BMI strata, age strata, and prior
  HF status (per prespecified subgroup analyses).

## Caveats
- Population is selected (prior CVD), so absolute risk reduction in
  primary-prevention obese adults is plausibly smaller than the
  trial's ~1.5 percentage-point absolute MACE reduction over ~40
  months.
- **High discontinuation rate** (~17%) means the per-protocol
  effect is larger than the intention-to-treat effect; but it also
  raises a real-world adherence question.
- Cost and access are non-trivial in many health systems.
- Long-term effects beyond 4 years (e.g. on body composition,
  bone, sarcopenia, all-cause mortality) remain under study.

## Practical takeaway
- For adults with prior CVD + obesity but without diabetes,
  semaglutide 2.4 mg/week reduces MACE by ~20% relative.
- For primary-prevention obese adults without prior CVD, the
  trial does not directly inform the decision; expect a smaller
  absolute risk reduction.
- GLP-1s are now in the same conversation as statins for primary
  cardiovascular prevention in obese patients, but the evidence
  base, side-effect profile, and cost are different and decisions
  should be individualised.
