# Sex-specific lifespan extension and weight loss (ITP reanalysis)

## Summary
Meta-analysis across the **DrugAge** database with a focus on
[[papers/itp-nia]]-generated cohorts. Tightens data quality (standardizes
dosing to ppm, records route, start-age, control lifespans, weight).

## Key findings
- In ITP-only studies, average lifespan extension is **5.5% in males**
  vs. **1.6% in females**.
- Effects range from **−7% to +26%** of control.
- **Weight loss correlates with lifespan extension in males** — a large
  share of apparent drug benefit may be mediated by caloric-restriction-like
  weight change.

## Why a CS person should care
Direct confounder to adjust for in any ITP reanalysis. Also: a public
well-curated DrugAge derivative that plays well with causal-inference
tooling.
