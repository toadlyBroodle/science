# Statins work for primary prevention too (CTT IPD meta-analysis, 27 RCTs)

## Summary
**Cholesterol Treatment Trialists' (CTT) Collaboration** individual-
participant-data meta-analysis of **27 randomised trials of statin
therapy**. Each **1 mmol/L (~39 mg/dL) reduction in LDL-C** produced
**~21% reduction in major vascular events** per year of treatment.
Crucially, the benefit was **maintained in low-risk subgroups**
(5-year vascular event risk <10%), demonstrating that statins are
not just a secondary-prevention drug.

## Why it matters
Settled the long-running primary-prevention debate. Statins reduce
events even in people without prior CVD or diabetes, with absolute
benefit proportional to baseline risk and LDL-C reduction. With
generic statins now available, this is one of the most cost-
effective preventive interventions in medicine, and remains
**systematically underprescribed** for primary prevention.

## Key findings
- 27 RCTs, 174,000+ participants pooled IPD.
- Each 1 mmol/L LDL-C reduction: ~21% lower major vascular events
  per year.
- Effect held across baseline LDL-C levels and risk strata.
- In the lowest-risk subgroup (5-yr vascular risk <10%): RR 0.62
  for major vascular events per 1 mmol/L LDL-C lowering.
- No excess of cancer, hemorrhagic stroke, or other serious adverse
  events.
- Myopathy / rhabdomyolysis risk small and dose-dependent.

## How to act on it
- Discuss with a clinician if your **apoB**
  ([[papers/sniderman-2011-apob-meta]]) or LDL-C is elevated,
  particularly with family history of early CVD.
- Generic statins (atorvastatin, rosuvastatin) are first-line.
- PCSK9 inhibitors (evolocumab, alirocumab) for those who can't
  reach target on statins.
- The benefit is dose-dependent: aim for the LDL-C / apoB target,
  not just "on a statin."

## Caveats
- Composite endpoint dominated by non-fatal MI; all-cause mortality
  benefit is smaller in absolute terms in primary prevention.
- 5-year time horizon for benefit; not for short-term symptomatic
  treatment.
- Side-effect profile (myalgia, diabetes incidence) needs
  individualized assessment.
