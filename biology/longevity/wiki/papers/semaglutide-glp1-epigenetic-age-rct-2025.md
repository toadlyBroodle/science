# First RCT of GLP-1 agonist with epigenetic-age outcomes

## Summary
**32-week double-blind RCT** of **semaglutide vs. placebo** in adults
with HIV-associated lipohypertrophy (semaglutide n=45, placebo n=39).
Multiple epigenetic clocks moved in the **rejuvenation direction**:
**PCGrimAge -3.1 yr, GrimAgeV2 -2.3 yr, PhenoAge -4.9 yr, DunedinPACE
~9% slower**. Multi-system organ-clock improvement (inflammation,
brain, cardiovascular, hepatic, renal axes).

## Why it matters
GLP-1 agonists (semaglutide, tirzepatide) are the most consequential
new drug class of the decade and were not previously evaluated as
**geroprotectors with biological-age endpoints**. This is the first
RCT to do so. If the effect replicates in a non-HIV cohort, GLP-1
agonists become a **plausible repurposing candidate** alongside
metformin and rapamycin ([[papers/pearl-rapamycin-2025]],
[[papers/itp-nia]]).

## Key findings
- N=84, 32 weeks, double-blind, placebo-controlled.
- PCGrimAge: -3.1 yr; GrimAgeV2: -2.3 yr; PhenoAge: -4.9 yr.
- DunedinPACE: ~9% slower aging rate.
- Organ-system clocks for inflammation, brain, cardiovascular,
  hepatic, renal all moved in the rejuvenation direction.
- Population (HIV with lipohypertrophy) has elevated baseline
  biological age, so absolute effect sizes may not transfer.

## Caveats
- Specific population (HIV-associated metabolic syndrome).
- Weight loss confounds biological-age improvement; needs
  weight-loss-matched comparator to disentangle.
- Preprint — peer review pending.
- Only a single trial; needs replication in healthy or general
  metabolic cohorts.
