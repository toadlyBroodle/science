# PRISMA review: NAD+ target engagement does not equal clinical benefit

## Summary
Ageing Research Reviews 2026. A PRISMA-guided systematic review of 113
studies (33 human intervention studies, 80 rodent studies) on NAD+
precursor supplementation (NMN, NR). Oral supplementation reliably
raised NAD+ and related metabolites and was well tolerated in humans,
but functional, metabolic, vascular, and healthspan outcomes were
heterogeneous and frequently null or endpoint-specific.

## Why it matters
Gives the wiki's [[topics/nad-mitophagy]] topic a rigorous,
null-leaning synthesis that quantifies the gap between biochemical
target engagement and clinical effect. It is consistent with the single
negative-endpoint RCT already covered
([[papers/nr-longcovid-2025]]) and supports the wiki's standing
recommendation against NR/NMN supplementation on current evidence.
Underlying human studies are mostly small RCTs with surrogate or
functional endpoints, not hard-endpoint trials.

## Key findings
- 113 studies synthesized: 33 human intervention, 80 rodent.
- Oral NMN/NR consistently achieved biochemical target engagement
  (raised NAD+/metabolites) and was well tolerated in humans.
- Functional, metabolic, vascular, and healthspan outcomes were
  heterogeneous and frequently null.
- Conclusion: reliable NAD+ elevation does not translate to consistent
  clinical ageing benefit.
