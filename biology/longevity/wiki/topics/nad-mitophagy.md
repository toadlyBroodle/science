# Mitochondrial health: NAD⁺, mitophagy, and bioenergetics

NAD⁺ is a central coenzyme in redox metabolism; levels decline with
age. Mitophagy is the selective autophagy of damaged mitochondria.
Mitochondrial biogenesis and quality control sit upstream of nearly
every aging hallmark. The supplements most often labelled
"mitochondrial" (NR, NMN, CoQ10, urolithin A) target distinct
sub-mechanisms and have very different evidence strengths.

## What the human RCTs actually show

### Mitophagy enhancement (positive, modest)
- [[papers/urolithin-a-immune-2025]] — urolithin A 1 g/day, RCT,
  immune-aging endpoints. Real but modest effect. Mechanism is
  specific (gut microbial metabolite of ellagitannins, induces
  PINK1/Parkin mitophagy). Sold OTC as Mitopure.

### NAD⁺ precursors (negative on clinical endpoints)
- [[papers/nr-longcovid-2025]] — nicotinamide riboside 2 g/day in
  long-COVID. Blood NAD⁺ rose; clinical endpoints did not.
- The broader pattern from NR/NMN trials: peripheral blood NAD⁺
  rises reliably; cardiovascular, metabolic, and muscle endpoints
  are largely null. Recommendation: do not supplement NR/NMN on
  current evidence.
- [[papers/nad-supplementation-aging-systematic-review-2026]] — PRISMA
  review of 113 studies (33 human, 80 rodent): reliable NAD⁺ target
  engagement, but functional/healthspan outcomes frequently null.
  Reinforces the no-supplement recommendation.

### CoQ10 (positive, indication-specific)
- [[papers/mortensen-2014-coq10-qsymbio]] — Q-SYMBIO RCT in chronic
  HF (NYHA III–IV), n=420, CoQ10 100 mg TID. Primary MACE HR 0.50
  (p=0.003), all-cause mortality HR 0.51 over 2 yr. **Specific to
  symptomatic heart failure, not generalisable to healthy adults.**

## Mitochondrial biogenesis: exercise is dominant

The largest mitochondrial intervention on the wiki is **endurance
training**. VO2max ([[papers/mandsager-2018-vo2max-mortality]]) is
in part a measure of skeletal-muscle mitochondrial density and
oxidative capacity. The Norwegian 4×4 protocol
([[papers/helgerud-2007-4x4-vo2max]]) raises VO2max via measurable
increases in stroke volume and (downstream) muscle mitochondrial
adaptations. Single supplements cannot match the mitochondrial
biogenesis effect of consistent endurance training.

## Honest summary for the longevity-supplement question

| Intervention | Evidence | Recommendation |
|--------------|----------|----------------|
| Endurance training | T7 mortality | Yes |
| Resistance + creatine | T7 biomarker | Yes |
| Urolithin A (Mitopure) | T6 RCT, modest | Reasonable adjunct |
| CoQ10 | T7 RCT in HF only | Yes if HF; no otherwise |
| NR / NMN | T6 RCT, null on endpoints | No |

## Hallmarks-of-aging context

Mitochondrial dysfunction is one of the canonical hallmarks of
aging. The wiki's [[topics/hallmarks-of-aging]] page situates this
within the broader framework. Mitochondrial decline is upstream of
several other hallmarks (cellular senescence, stem cell exhaustion,
loss of proteostasis), which is part of why exercise has such broad
downstream effects.
